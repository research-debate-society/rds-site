Proxy Wars in the Pak-Afghan Borderland: Understanding the Sectarian Problem in the Merged District of Kurram, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa
Dr Noreen Naseer — Assistant Professor, Dept. of Political Science, University of Peshawar
Dr Iftikhar Firdous — Founder & Editor, The Khorasan Diary; PhD Psycholinguistics, UC, USA
Mr Abdul Rehman — Undergraduate Student, Islamia College Peshawar

To understand the sectarian problem in the merged district of Kurram, both primary and secondary sources are employed to assess and analyse Kurram's strategic position and security mosaic. The traditional understanding of its causes and effects does not offer a solution-based analysis. The last two decades have metastasised this bloody sectarian conflict from a local to an international conflict. Characterising the conflict in Kurram as merely a sectarian clash or a land dispute is not only reductionist but also obscures the deeper structural drivers of violence, including persistent governance failures and the entrenched presence of militant organisations, compounded by proxy dynamics resulting from years of involvement by neighbouring countries.

Guidelines for Submissions
01
What is a Research Article?

A research article presents original findings from primary research — including surveys, interviews, experiments, archival work, or quantitative data analysis. It follows a structured format: introduction, methodology, findings, discussion, and conclusion.

02
What We're Looking For

We welcome undergraduate and postgraduate research in any field — political science, history, economics, social sciences, natural sciences. Your methodology must be clearly described and your claims proportionate to your evidence.

03
Our Review Process

Research articles go through editorial review before publication. We may request revisions for clarity, citation completeness, or methodological transparency. We aim to respond to all submissions within 7–10 days.
